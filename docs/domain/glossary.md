# Hold'em Glossary

This glossary is a quick-reference index for common Texas Hold'em terms used in this repository's domain docs.

For full rules and examples, see [`texas-holdem.md`](texas-holdem.md) and [`heads-up-nlhe.md`](heads-up-nlhe.md).

## A

- **Action**: a player's decision opportunity or the move they make, such as fold, check, call, bet, or raise.
- **All-in**: committing all remaining chips to the pot.

## B

- **Bet**: the first voluntary wager on a betting round.
- **Big blind**: the larger forced preflop blind.
- **Blank**: a community card that appears unlikely to change relative hand strength materially.
- **Board**: the shared community cards.
- **Button**: the dealer marker that determines positional order.
- **Burn card**: a face-down card discarded before dealing community cards.

## C

- **Call**: matching the current wager, or committing all remaining chips if the player cannot cover the full amount.
- **Check**: declining to bet when no wager is facing the player.
- **Community cards**: the shared board cards available to all remaining players.
- **Current wager**: the amount a player must have committed on the current street to be fully matched.

## D

- **Dealer button**: see **Button**.
- **Dominated hand**: a hand that is at a strong disadvantage because an opponent can have a better version of the same made hand or draw type.
- **Draw**: a hand that is not yet made but can improve to a stronger hand on later streets.

## F

- **Flop**: the first three community cards, and the betting round that follows them.
- **Fold**: surrendering the hand and forfeiting any claim to the pot.
- **Full house**: a hand containing three cards of one rank and two cards of another rank.
- **Flush**: a hand containing five cards of the same suit.

## H

- **Hand**: depending on context, either a complete deal of Hold'em or a player's resulting five-card poker combination.
- **Heads-up**: a hand or match with exactly two players.
- **Hole cards**: the two private cards dealt to a player.

## K

- **Kicker**: a side card used to break ties between otherwise similar hands.

## N

- **No-limit**: a betting structure in which a player may wager up to their full remaining stack.

## O

- **Odd chip**: a leftover chip when an integer-valued pot cannot be split evenly between tied hands; exact assignment is often house-rule-dependent.
- **Open** or **opening bet**: the first bet made on a betting round.
- **Outs**: unseen cards that would likely improve a hand to the likely winner.

## P

- **Pair**: two cards of the same rank.
- **Position**: where a player acts relative to other players on a betting round.
- **Pot**: the total chips wagered in the hand.
- **Preflop**: the betting round that occurs after hole cards are dealt and before any community cards are revealed.

## R

- **Raise**: increasing the current wager after a bet or raise.
- **Rank**: the value of a card, from `2` through `A`; also used for hand category or hand strength in context.
- **River**: the fifth community card, and the betting round that follows it.

## S

- **Set**: three of a kind made using a pocket pair plus one matching board card.
- **Short all-in**: an all-in wager for less than a full legal raise.
- **Showdown**: the final comparison of hands when more than one player remains.
- **Small blind**: the smaller forced preflop blind.
- **Straight**: five consecutively ranked cards.
- **Straight flush**: five consecutive cards of the same suit.
- **Street**: a betting phase of the hand: preflop, flop, turn, or river.
- **Suit**: clubs, diamonds, hearts, or spades.

## T

- **Three of a kind**: a hand with three cards of the same rank.
- **To call**: the amount a player must add on the current street to match the current wager.
- **Top pair**: pairing the highest-ranked card currently on the board.
- **Turn**: the fourth community card, and the betting round that follows it.
- **Two pair**: a hand with two cards of one rank and two cards of another rank.

## U

- **Uncalled wager**: the portion of a bet or raise that is not matched because the opponent folds; it is returned to the bettor rather than awarded through showdown.

## V

- **Value bet**: a bet made with the expectation of being called by worse hands often enough to profit.
